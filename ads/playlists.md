Playlists can be private or public.

Private playlists are only visible to the playlist owner and their selected "accounts to share to" (emails they entered) and is not even visible or searchable to people not shared to. 

Public playlists are visible and searchable to everyone including unauthorized users and will show the owner's name and profile picture. Logged in users can "follow" a playlist to get updates when it's, well, updated; or "save" a playlist to add it to their library for easy access; or "fork" a playlist to make a copy of that playlist in their library but they themselves is the owner of that playlist (there should be a "link" back to the original forked playlist to have some sense of origins and community though), forked playlists can be public or private independently from the original playlist.

Private playlists can be made public with mostly no side effects, but making a public playlist private will render all forks to be cut from the playlist (they will be considered original playlist, not forked), remove all follows, and is generally a dick move so is discouraged. Also, users who previously saved that playlist can still see that playlist as usual.

Users can fork a fork playlist fine. Not much ambiguity here.

Playlists themselves are a playable entity collection.
